The organization ensures design controls are applied across the lifecycle for new, modified, and legacy devices through several documented procedures, plans, and measures specifically for HAL 4.0. The key elements include:

1. **Design and Development Procedures**: The Design and Development Procedure (DND01) outlines the processes and requirements for developing software items associated with risk control measures. This procedure sets out the phases of the design process and specifies the necessary management oversight through Phase Gates, complying with global regulatory requirements such as US FDA 21 CFR 820 and ISO 13485 [Regulatory / D70036 HAL 4.0 Software Description Report China Submission Rev 03 .docx.pdf | Page: 23].

2. **Change Management**: Change requests are managed through a detailed process documented in procedures such as DND04F01 Change Request and DND19 Software Configuration Management Procedure. These ensure changes are approved and properly controlled, maintaining traceability and compliance [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001 IEC60601-1 Ed.3.2 HAL4.0_30_APR_2026 Rev 1.pdf | Page: 288-289].

3. **Risk Management**: Risk management is integral to the lifecycle management of HAL 4.0, with procedures like RSK01 Medical Device Risk Management ensuring that risks are controlled and verified through comprehensive risk management reports and plans. The Risk Management File is updated as necessary to reflect changes in risks due to modifications or new designs [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001 IEC60601-1 Ed.3.2 HAL4.0_30_APR_2026 Rev 1.pdf | Page: 292-293].

4. **Verification and Validation**: Verification and validation plans (DND08T01 Verification Plan and DND09T01 Validation Plan) are included in the Design and Development Plan. These plans specify the requirements for testing and verifying changes and new designs, ensuring that all modifications meet the predefined acceptance criteria and comply with design control requirements [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / 104993953MIN-001 - HAL4.0 - IEC 60601-1.pdf | Page: 243-244].

5. **Configuration Management**: Configuration items are documented and controlled as part of the configuration management procedures. This includes standard libraries and vendor software, ensuring that all versions are traceable and managed appropriately throughout the device's lifecycle [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / 104993953MIN-001 - HAL4.0 - IEC 60601-1.pdf | Page: 298-299].

These processes demonstrate that the organization implements a structured approach to design controls that spans the entire lifecycle, encompassing initial development through legacy device management.
