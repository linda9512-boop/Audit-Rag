The evidence suggests that traceability is maintained across product families and versions for the Halcyon 4.0 radiotherapy system. The traceability is documented in several excerpts, as follows:

1. **Traceability Plan**: The document [Design Transfer / Manufacturing Documentation / D58685-Rev04-HAL4.0 Traceability Plan.pdf] outlines the traceability plan for the HAL4.0 product platform, which includes the Halcyon 4.0 and Ethos Radiotherapy System 4.0 [source, page 4]. This document specifies that all medical device components must adhere to traceability requirements, with any future changes necessitating an update to the plan [source, page 4]. Additionally, traceability is evaluated and approved by various departments, ensuring comprehensive oversight [source, pages 5-6].

2. **Verification and Validation Summary**: Various validation summary reports detail how traceability is recorded, including mapping requirements to validation test outcomes. For example, the [Design Validation / Verification and Validation Summary Reports / D68014 HAL 4.0 Validation Summary Report Rev 02.docx.pdf] advises referring to the Validation Trace Matrix for validation test results and associated user needs and risk control measures [source, page 11].

3. **Design and Development Documentation**: The system's design and development documentation, including pervasive references to a "Design Trace Matrix," ensures that all requirements are traceable through the document matrix, offering a structured path from requirements, to implementation, to testing [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / 104993953MIN-001 - HAL4.0 - IEC 60601-1.pdf | page 293].

4. **Risk Management Documentation**: The HAL 4.0 Risk Control Measures Trace Matrix [source: Risk Management / Risk Management Reports / D68779 HAL 4.0 Risk Control Measures Trace Matrix rev01.pdf] further supports the traceability framework by cross-referencing verification measures and risk control measures [source, many pages].

5. **Cybersecurity Traceability Analysis**: The [Risk Management / Risk Management Reports / D68683 HAL 4.0 Cybersecurity Traceability Analysis Rev 01.pdf] also contributes to the traceability effort by mapping cybersecurity requirements and confirming their addressal through risk management processes [source, page 8].

In each of these documents, there is a structured approach to maintaining traceability across different components and iterations of the HAL4.0 system, corroborating that traceability is maintained for the product line. These approaches include detailed process documentation, oversight from various departments, and consistent updates when changes occur, ensuring a robust traceability mechanism across product families and versions.
