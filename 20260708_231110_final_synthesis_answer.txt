2. **Risk Management**: Risk management is a consistent part of the lifecycle management. The RSK01 Medical Device Risk Management procedure is used in conjunction with the DND01 Design and Development Procedure to evaluate risks during all phases of development, as well as the management of residual risks [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001, Page: 292-293].

3. **Traceability and Verification**: The design and development process includes traceability from system requirements to risk control measures, ensuring that all changes are documented and verified. 4. **Configuration Management**: Software configuration management is documented in the DND04T01 Design and Development Plan, ensuring all configuration items are properly identified and controlled throughout the lifecycle [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001, Page: 287-288].

5. **Change Management**: Changes to the software and configuration items are managed through a formal change request process, documented in DND04F01 Change Request and related procedural documents to ensure all changes are verified and approved [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001, Page: 293-294].

6. **Verification and Validation**: The development process includes rigorous verification and validation activities to ensure all design outputs meet established requirements and any failures are effectively addressed [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D101277 106485676MIN-001, Page: 226-227; 104993953MIN-001, Page: 278-279].

These processes delineate how the organization systematically applies design control procedures to maintain compliance and manage risks across the lifecycle for new, modified, and legacy devices such as the Halcyon 4.0.
