Audit question: Are country-specific requirements addressed, such as FDA DHF, EU Technical File, or Health Canada STED?
Chunks used: 27
================================================================================
The documents do provide evidence addressing country-specific requirements for the Halcyon 4.0 (HAL 4.0) radiotherapy system:

1. **FDA DHF (Design History File) Requirements:**
   - Compliance with FDA regulations is addressed. The HAL 4.0 is designed to meet various FDA regulations, including 21 CFR part 801, 803, 806, and 807, which cover labeling, medical device reporting, corrections and removals, and establishment registration and device listing for manufacturers [Regulatory / Applicable Standards / D45070 Hal 4 Applicable Product Standards Rev 2.docx.pdf | Page: 6].

2. **EU Technical File:**
   - HAL 4.0 is designed to comply with EU regulations, specifically the MDR 2017/745 Medical Device Regulation. The document mentions the Medical Device Directive (MDD) compliance and commitment to international and European standards [Regulatory / Applicable Standards / D45070 Hal 4 Applicable Product Standards Rev 2.docx.pdf | Page: 6].
   - The regulatory plan indicates that HAL 4.0 shall comply with standards and regulations, and includes country-specific subsections for EU requirements [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 9].

3. **Health Canada STED (Summary Technical Documentation):**
   - Compliance with Health Canada regulations is addressed in the document titled CMDR SOR/98-282, mentioning the Health Canada Safety Effectiveness and Labeling Requirements [Regulatory / Applicable Standards / D45070 Hal 4 Applicable Product Standards Rev 2.docx.pdf | Page: 6].
   - Additionally, the documentation states that all relevant information is available in both English and French to meet the Canadian requirements regarding language [Regulatory / International Submission Dossier / D70060 HAL 4.0 GSPR Checklist Rev2.docx.pdf | Page: 40].

These excerpts explicitly state that the regulatory requirements for FDA, EU, and Health Canada are addressed for the Halcyon 4.0 device, identifying specific standards and regulations it complies with.
