Audit question: What criteria are used to classify a product as a medical device under applicable regulatory jurisdictions?
Chunks used: 200
================================================================================
The criteria used to classify a product as a medical device under various applicable regulatory jurisdictions for the Halcyon 4.0 radiotherapy system are as follows:

1. **United States**: The HAL 4.0 is classified as a Class II medical device under 21 CFR §892.5050, which covers linear accelerator systems used for medical purposes [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

2. **European Union**: Under the EU Medical Device Regulation (EU) 2017/745, HAL 4.0 is classified as Class IIb. This classification takes into account factors such as the nature of the device and its intended use, as per MDR Annex VIII Classification Criteria, Rule 9 [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

3. **Canada**: The HAL 4.0 is classified as a Class III medical device according to the Canadian Medical Device Regulations (SOR/98-282), following Schedule 1, Rule 8. This classification reflects the risk-based approach taken by Canada in assessing medical devices [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

4. **Japan**: In Japan, HAL 4.0 is classified as Class III, aligning with the Act on Securing Quality, Efficacy, and Safety of Pharmaceuticals, Medical Devices, Regenerative and Cellular Therapy Products, Gene Therapy Products, and Cosmetics, particularly Article 2, Paragraph 5 [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

5. **China**: The device falls under Class III as per the National Medical Products Administration (NMPA), specifically within the medical device classification category 05-01-01 for medical electron accelerators [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

6. **Australia**: The device is classified as Class IIb under the Therapeutic Goods (Medical Devices) Regulations 2002 – Schedule 2, reflecting its intended use in therapeutic settings [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

7. **Brazil**: HAL 4.0 is considered a Class III device according to Resolution RDC 185/2001, indicating a higher classification due to potential risks associated with its use [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 5].

For all these jurisdictions, the classifications are based on a combination of the device's intended use, its potential risks, and specific regulatory criteria as established by each country's medical device regulatory body.
