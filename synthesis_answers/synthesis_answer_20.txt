Audit question: How does the organization ensure updates to regulatory requirements are reflected in technical documentation?
Chunks used: 37
================================================================================
The excerpts provide partial evidence on how the organization ensures updates to regulatory requirements are reflected in technical documentation for the Halcyon 4.0 radiotherapy system. 

1. **Regulatory Responsibility**: The project team is tasked with ensuring that regulatory requirements, including national language requirements, are included within the design and development plans according to current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMPs) and national legislation. Regulatory submissions might require the product to meet additional standards, and these are documented in country-specific subsections of regulatory plans [Regulatory / Regulatory Plans / D54978 HAL 4.0 Regulatory Plan Rev 5.0.pdf | Page: 9].

2. **Standards and Updates**: The "Applicable Standards" document lists the standards the device must comply with. These standards are used to update design and development plans and are tracked for any changes that could affect product compliance. When new or updated standards are identified, they influence updates in the technical documentation [Regulatory / Applicable Standards / D47457 HAL 4.0 APS Record Rev 04.docx.pdf | Page: 4].

3. **Document Management and Archives**: There is a process for finalizing and archiving project documents, which encompasses the review and approval of documentation to ensure it meets required regulations and standards, such as the "Final UAP noting the final UA versions" [Design Output Specification / Labeling / D65651 Halcyon-Ethos 4.0 IFU (P1058124-001-A) User Assistance Checklist Rev01.docx.pdf | Pages: 4, 6].

4. **Change Requests**: Necessary updates to technical documentation due to regulatory changes are managed through change requests. These might include risk assessments and regression tests to ensure compliance before rolling out changes [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D78333 Ethos 4.0 Description of interoperability Study Rev01.docx.pdf | Page: 21].

5. **Validation and Verification**: The validation plans include processes for updating and validating service documentation as part of new software releases, ensuring that technical documentation reflects the latest compliance requirements [Design Validation / Verification and Validation Plans / D54550 HAL 4.0 Validation Plan Rev 05.docx.pdf | Pages: 11-12].
