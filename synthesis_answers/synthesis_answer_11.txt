Audit question: How is risk integrated into design and development activities?
Chunks used: 37
================================================================================
The provided excerpts offer the following insights into how risk is integrated into the design and development activities for the Halcyon 4.0 radiotherapy system:

1. **Integration of Risk into Planning and Development:**
   - The Design and Development Plan for HAL 4.0 incorporates risk management by detailing activities, deliverables, and justifications for exceptions. Risk analysis begins at the elaboration phase with the identification of main hazards, including potential misuse and off-label use cases [Regulatory / D70036 HAL 4.0 Software Description Report China Submission Rev 03 .docx.pdf, Page: 24].
   - Risk control measures are identified and verified during product development. These measures are integrated into the system design and validated as part of the verification and validation processes [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D44094_HAL4.0_CER_Rev05.pdf, Page: 38].

2. **Risk Management Process:**
   - The HAL 4.0 Risk Management Plan is aligned with standards like ISO 14971:2012, ensuring thorough and systematic risk assessment and management throughout the product lifecycle [Regulatory / D70036 HAL 4.0 Software Description Report China Submission Rev 03 .docx.pdf, Page: 23].
   - The risk management process involves identifying inconsistencies, design flaws, and potential failures, with a focus on architecture, design, and components. The results are incorporated into a Risk Management Report (RMR) [Risk Management / Risk Management Plans / D59555 HAL 4.0 Risk Management Plan rev03.pdf, Page: 7].

3. **Validation and Control Measures:**
   - Risk control measures, which mitigate design-related risks, are tested during the design validation phase. These measures ensure user needs and safety requirements are met [Design Validation / Verification and Validation Plans / D54550 HAL 4.0 Validation Plan Rev 05.docx.pdf, Page: 36].
   - Usability and validation testing are performed to ensure that human factor risks are adequately controlled, and design mitigators are evaluated for effectiveness [Design Validation / Test Plans / D67097 HAL 4.0 Summative Usability Evaluation Plan Rev01.docx.pdf, Page: 13].

4. **Continuous Risk Monitoring and Adaption:**
   - Risk management is an iterative process that continues throughout the product lifecycle, including post-market surveillance and updates in response to new risks or regulatory changes [Regulatory / International Submission Dossier / D70060 HAL 4.0 GSPR Checklist Rev2.docx.pdf, Page: 45].

5. **Cybersecurity and Maintenance Risks:**
   - The risk management strategy also encompasses cybersecurity risks, with ongoing assessments and updates as required to address vulnerabilities [Regulatory / Test Reports - Regulatory Compliance / D75414 HAL 4.0 Cybersecurity Summary Report China Submission Rev 01.pdf, Page: 14].

Concrete device-specific determinations:
- The HAL 4.0 risk management documentation includes detailed assessments of hazards, potential misuse, and the effectiveness of risk control measures within the system's design process, which are aligned with ISO standards [Regulatory / D70036 HAL 4.0 Software Description Report China Submission Rev 03 .docx.pdf, Page: 23].
- There is a statement that the overall residual risk for HAL 4.0, after applying risk control measures, is considered acceptable [Regulatory / D70036 HAL 4.0 Software Description Report China Submission Rev 03 .docx.pdf, Page: 23].
